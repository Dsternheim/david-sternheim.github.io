About Me

My name is David Sternheim. I currently work as a software engineer developing spring boot microservices. I attended Stony Brook University where I graduated with a B.E. in Computer Engineering. My passion for software development stems from a relentless desire to solve the toughest problems we face today.

With rapid continuous open source software it's amazing to witness so many new frameworks and technology arise to industry standards. The only problem with this velocity is that to advance our careers as developers we can sometimes get lost in the shuffle. And by that I mean these new frameworks appear on tons of job postings so the amount of time using these frameworks is limited before jumping ship to the next shiny new technology.

I'm a firm believer in focusing on strong fundamentals because it's the key to navigating these evergrowing field of innovative technology. That being said, I do strive to continuously learn new things each day because I believe that is the true key to success not just in software development, but life as well. That and clean elegant code (thanks Uncle Bob).

We live in an age where technology has a role in nearly everything we do. Being able to leverage the capabilities of technology to further society is what makes software development so amazing. And as far as we've made it, there's still so much untapped potential such as advanced virtual assistants. I for one cannot wait to be a part of the next golden age of technological advancements.
